Parity

Solve a leg of put-call parity. Two models, set in Settings > Parity model.

Real model (default) — matches the parity-zetamac trainer. Two identities are pinned each round:
  C + P = straddle
  C − P = S − K + r/c
where r/c is a small signed carry term (interest minus dividends, times time). Compute C−P from S−K+r/c, then add or subtract the straddle and halve. Random leg blanked each round — can include the straddle or r/c itself.

Simple model — zero-rate C − P = S − K. Easier per round but trains the same sign-flip reflex. Optional discounting toggle adds an explicit factor e−rT.

Market Making

You are shown a fair value (or enough to compute one via parity); quote a two-sided market by entering bid then ask. Each quote scores 0–100:

  • Width score: tighter is better, up to the spread target (Tight = 2, Medium = 4).
  • Centre score: mid (B+A)/2 should equal fair; falls off as mid drifts.
  • Pick-off: bidding above fair or offering below fair is instant zero. The rule that gives the drill its tension — you cannot just collapse the spread to zero.

Show size toggle adds a requested-size line each round. The effective target widens with size as teff = t × (1 + log10(size)), and width scoring becomes symmetric around it: quoting too tight on a 500-lot trade is now a fail, not a win. Pick-off still trumps everything.

Position P&L

A single-leg position at expiry. Given side (long/short), instrument (call/put), strike K, premium p, and terminal stock ST, compute total P&L:

  • Long call: max(ST−K, 0) − p
  • Long put: max(K−ST, 0) − p
  • Short call: p − max(ST−K, 0)
  • Short put: p − max(K−ST, 0)

Multiply by quantity and, if the multiplier toggle is on, by the contract multiplier (100 default for equity options). The drill mixes all four side×instrument combinations and a mix of ITM/OTM/near-money outcomes so you cannot pattern-match. The sign on the short premium is the most common error: short means you receive premium but pay out the intrinsic, so a short call on an ITM expiry is negative.

Strike spacing setting

Round: strikes step on multiples of 5 (with small cent offsets in Simple mode). Fine: strikes can land anywhere near spot. Subtle difficulty knob — Round is closer to a real strike ladder, Fine adds extra mental load. If unsure, leave on Round.

History

Every finished session is saved per-mode with a trend chart. The dashed yellow line is a least-squares fit through the points — the slope tells you whether you are actually improving, more reliably than any single session. Stable around 15+ per session is a stronger SIG-prep signal than one lucky run at 25.
